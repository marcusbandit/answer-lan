Topo / Outdoor bouldering near Lyon

Free blocs, a few metres tall, and where to rent a crashpad.

No ropes, no belayer: just short natural boulders you can climb ground-up, with a fall mat under you. Lyon has real granite and limestone blocs in the Monts d'Or on its doorstep, and the Alpine forests an hour out. Below: the free bouldering spots, then who rents pads.

Monts d'Or blocs

The classic Lyon home crags: short limestone walls and free-standing boulders scattered across the hills right on the city's doorstep, most just a few metres tall. Free to climb, well documented in the FFME du Rhône guidebook and on theCrag.

Curis-au-Mont-d'Or

Limestone in the Monts d'Or just north of the city, reachable by public transport. Mostly a roped crag, but the sector has plenty of low sections and boulders to work ground-up when you keep it short. A good no-car half-day if you bring your own pad.

Alpine forest blocs (foothills)

An hour towards the Alps, the forests open up to granite and sandstone boulder fields with proper landings. Bigger day out, but the reward is quiet, low-to-mid-height blocs. Lyon Secret rounds up the outdoor options in the wider region.

Renting a crashpad in Lyon

bouldering-specific fall mats

Crashpad rental is niche in France, so plan ahead. The most reliable routes are outdoor-gear shops with a rental counter (a common weekend option is a foldable pad for roughly 12-18€/day), or peer-to-peer gear rental. Call ahead to confirm stock before you drive out.

Decathlon Location (multiple Lyon stores)

Decathlon's rental service (Decathlon Location / Rent) covers a growing catalogue of outdoor gear, and Simond crashpads appear at selected larger stores. Check the Lyon-area shops (Confluence, Part-Dieu, Vaise) online before you go, or buy an entry Simond pad outright from around 100€ if you will boulder often.

~15€per day, if stocked

Expe / local mountain shops

Specialist climbing and mountain shops around Lyon (Expe, Snowleader pickup points, independent grimpe stores) are the surest bet for a real bouldering pad and beta on where to use it. A phone call to ask "vous louez des crashpads?" saves a wasted trip.

Askcall to confirm

Peer-to-peer gear rental

Sites and apps for renting sports gear from locals (Rentle, AlloVoisins, Le Bon Coin listings) regularly turn up crashpads in the Lyon area, usually cheaper than a shop and often collectable the same day. Search "crashpad Lyon" or "matelas escalade".

~10€per day, local

Guided outing with gear included

If sourcing a pad is too much hassle, a guided bouldering half-day through Equilibre Vertical brings the pads (and spotting) for you, and takes you straight to the good low blocs.

Bouldering safe outdoors

  • Bring at least one pad, ideally two, and a spotter: even a 3 m fall onto rock hurts without a mat under you.
  • Grab the regional topo Escalade autour de Lyon (FFME du Rhône, 2018, 20€) for boulder heights, landings and approaches.
  • Limestone dries slowly: after rain, the blocs stay greasy for a day or two, so check the forecast.

No pad, no car?

  • Equilibre Vertical runs guided outings with pads included, adapting the blocs to your level.
  • For a wet-weather backup, indoor bouldering halls in town (Climb Up and others) rent shoes and need no pad of your own.
